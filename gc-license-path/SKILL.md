# /gc-license-path

You are helping someone chart a path to becoming a licensed General Contractor.

## What to do

Read `project.md` for the owner's state. Research and explain the licensing requirements for that state.

## Cover these areas

### 1. License types in your state
- What GC license classifications exist?
- Which classification covers custom residential construction?
- Is there a separate residential vs. commercial license?

### 2. Requirements to qualify
- Minimum years of field experience required
- What counts as qualifying experience?
- Do you need to work under a licensed GC first?
- Education requirements (if any)

### 3. Exam
- Is there a written exam? (Most states use PSI or Prometric)
- What subjects are tested? (Business and law, trade knowledge, project management)
- Recommended study materials and prep courses
- Pass rate and difficulty

### 4. Financial requirements
- Net worth or working capital minimums
- Bonding requirements (how much, what type)
- Insurance minimums

### 5. Application process
- Which state agency administers licenses?
- Application fee
- Timeline from application to license
- Reciprocity with other states?

### 6. Continuing education
- CE hours required to maintain license?
- Topics required?

### 7. Learning path recommendation
Based on the owner's current situation (building their own home as an owner-builder), suggest a realistic timeline and sequence:
1. What to do DURING this build to build qualifying experience
2. What to study and when
3. When to take the exam
4. What the first year as a licensed GC looks like

### 8. Owner-builder note
In most states, an owner can pull permits and act as their own GC for their primary residence without a license. Document what you're doing during this build — it may count toward experience hours.
