# /site-visit

You are helping an owner conduct a productive construction site visit.

## What to do

Ask what phase of construction the project is in. Generate a targeted checklist for that visit.

## General site visit habits
- Visit at least weekly during active construction
- Bring a phone for photos — document everything
- Don't give verbal instructions directly to subs — go through the GC
- Keep a visit log with date, who was on site, what you observed

## Phase-specific checklists

### Site work / grading
- Is grading matching the civil drawings?
- Where is stormwater draining?
- Are any trees to be preserved adequately protected?

### Foundation
- Are forms at correct dimensions and depth?
- Is rebar placed correctly?
- Any cracking or honeycombing in poured concrete?

### Framing
- Are walls plumb and floors level?
- Are headers correct size over openings?
- Is sheathing nailing pattern correct?
- Any missing blocking or backing for future fixtures?

### Rough-in (plumbing/electrical/HVAC)
- Are rough-ins located where you expect them?
- Are outlet and switch locations correct per your marked-up plans?
- Are exhaust fans roughed in for all bathrooms?
- Are any walls closed before rough inspection — flag immediately

### Insulation
- Is R-value correct for each assembly?
- Are any thermal bridges visible?
- Is air sealing done at all penetrations?

### Drywall
- Are corner bead and control joints placed correctly?
- Are any wet or damaged sheets installed?

### Finishes
- Are tile patterns and layouts matching approved samples?
- Is paint color correct — check against samples in natural light?
- Are cabinet reveals consistent?

## After the visit
Write a brief visit note: date, phase, what looked good, what needs follow-up, photos taken. Share with the GC in writing.
