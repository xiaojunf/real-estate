# /builder-due-diligence

You are helping an owner vet a contractor before hiring them.

## What to do

Ask for the builder's name, license number, and state. Then guide the owner through a complete due diligence process.

## Verification checklist

### 1. License verification
- Go to your state's contractor licensing board website
- Search by name and license number
- Confirm: license is active, correct classification, no disciplinary actions
- Check expiration date

### 2. Insurance verification
- Request a Certificate of Insurance (COI) directly from their insurer
- Verify: General Liability ($1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate minimum)
- Verify: Workers Compensation (required if they have employees)
- Confirm you are named as additional insured on the GL policy

### 3. Lien and judgment search
- Search county recorder / court records for liens filed by their subs or suppliers
- Search for civil judgments in the county they work in
- A pattern of liens = not paying subs = your project is at risk

### 4. References
- Request 3-5 references from projects completed in the last 2 years
- Call them (don't just email) and ask:
  - Did they finish on budget? If not, by how much and why?
  - Did they finish on schedule? If not, by how long and why?
  - How did they handle problems or mistakes?
  - Would you hire them again?
  - Can I visit the completed project?

### 5. Portfolio review
- Visit at least one completed project in person if possible
- Look for: quality of trim work, straight lines, tight joints, clean finish details
- Ask about similar projects (same size, complexity, finish level as yours)

### 6. Financial health
- How long have they been in business under this entity?
- Do they have a physical office or just a truck?
- Will they provide a list of current active projects? (Overloaded builders are a risk)
- Do they use a dedicated project manager or is the owner running everything?

### 7. Subcontractor relationships
- Do they use consistent subs they have long relationships with?
- Or do they low-bid each sub on every project? (Signals race-to-bottom quality)

### 8. Red flags summary
Flag any of the following:
- License expired, suspended, or wrong classification
- No workers comp (liability falls on owner)
- Refuses to provide COI direct from insurer
- History of mechanic's liens filed against them
- References won't call back or give vague answers
- No completed projects you can visit
- Business entity less than 2 years old with no verifiable history

## Output
Produce a HIRE / DO NOT HIRE / PROCEED WITH CAUTION recommendation with specific evidence for each factor.
