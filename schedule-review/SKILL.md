# /schedule-review

You are helping an owner review and critique a construction schedule.

## What to do

Ask the user to share the schedule (paste it, describe it, or list the milestones and durations).

## Review areas

### 1. Completeness
- Are all major phases represented?
- Are permit timelines included (not just construction)?
- Are owner decision deadlines shown (selections due dates)?

### 2. Sequencing logic
- Does the sequence follow correct construction order?
- Are there any trades shown starting before prerequisite work is done?
- Are inspections shown before work that requires them is scheduled to close?

### 3. Duration reality check
Flag any durations that seem unrealistic:
- Foundation: typically 2-4 weeks (pour + cure)
- Framing a custom home: 4-8 weeks depending on size
- Rough-in (plumbing + electrical + HVAC): 4-6 weeks
- Insulation + drywall: 3-5 weeks
- Finishes (cabinets, flooring, tile, paint): 6-12 weeks
- Punchlist and closeout: 2-4 weeks
- Total custom home: typically 12-18 months from permit to CO

### 4. Float and contingency
- Is there schedule contingency built in?
- What happens to the end date if framing runs 2 weeks late?
- What are the critical path items?

### 5. Weather and seasonal risks
- Does the schedule account for weather delays?
- Are any weather-sensitive activities (concrete, roofing, exterior paint) scheduled in risky seasons?

### 6. Owner decision dates
- When do you need to finalize: cabinets, tile, fixtures, flooring, paint colors?
- Are these dates clearly shown and realistic (lead times accounted for)?

### 7. Red flags
- Schedule that is significantly shorter than typical without explanation
- No owner milestones shown
- No permit duration shown (permits often take 4-12 weeks)
- No contingency buffer before move-in date

## Output
A marked-up version of the schedule with specific questions and concerns for the builder.
