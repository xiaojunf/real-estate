# /inspection-prep

You are helping an owner prepare for a specific construction inspection.

## What to do

Ask which inspection is coming up. Then provide a targeted checklist of what to verify before the inspector arrives, what to watch during the inspection, and what to do after.

## Supported inspections

### Footing / foundation inspection
Before inspector arrives:
- Verify forms are at correct depth per soils report and code
- Rebar placed and tied per structural drawings
- No dirt or debris in forms
- Correct concrete mix ordered

What inspector checks: depth, dimensions, rebar placement, soil bearing

### Framing inspection
Before inspector arrives:
- All framing complete including blocking, headers, and shear walls
- Temporary bracing in place
- Hurricane/seismic hardware installed per drawings
- All penetrations through top plates sleeved

What inspector checks: spans, headers, bearing, shear walls, connection hardware

### Rough plumbing inspection
Before inspector arrives:
- All drain, waste, vent (DWV) lines visible and accessible
- Pressure test on supply lines (typically 80 PSI for 15 min)
- Vent stack visible above roof

### Rough electrical inspection
Before inspector arrives:
- All boxes installed at correct heights
- Wires secured and protected at penetrations
- Panel installed and labeled (breakers not required yet)
- AFCI/GFCI locations correct per code

### Rough mechanical (HVAC) inspection
Before inspector arrives:
- Duct work installed and supported
- Equipment locations confirmed
- Combustion air provisions if applicable

### Insulation inspection
Before inspector arrives:
- All rough-in inspections passed (do NOT insulate until this)
- Correct R-values per energy code
- Air sealing at all penetrations complete

### Drywall / wallboard inspection (some jurisdictions)
- Fastener spacing correct
- Fire-rated assemblies properly detailed

### Final inspection
This is the big one — inspector verifies everything:
- All systems operational (plumbing, electrical, HVAC)
- Smoke and CO detectors installed and functional
- Handrails and guardrails per code
- Exterior grading drains away from foundation
- Address numbers visible from street
- Garage door auto-reverse functional
- GFCI outlets test correctly

## After every inspection
- Get the inspection card signed or get written confirmation
- If there are corrections: get them in writing, understand whose responsibility they are
- Do not cover or proceed past any failed inspection without re-inspection and sign-off

## Learning note
After each inspection, note what the inspector flagged. Patterns tell you where your subs are weak.
